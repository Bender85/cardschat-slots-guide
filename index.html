#1 - how to play

Learning baccarat is dead simple and we can teach you everything you need to know in just a few minutes. Just remember there are only three main options to bet on and once you’ve placed your bet you can sit back, relax, and let the dealer play out the hand. Let’s take a closer look at baccarat basics.

Game Objective

As far as you’re concerned, the goal in baccarat is to make winning bets. You can bet on the player hand, the banker hand or a tie. The dealer takes care of everything and the hand that comes closest to nine points wins..

Rules of Gameplay

Unless you’re playing in a special high-stakes room, you’re probably playing mini-baccarat or Punto Banco. In full baccarat the players actually have to deal the cards. In mini-bac and Punto Banco the dealer does everything for you and it’s not necessary to understand all the rules.

Let’s run through an example hand quickly to show you what we mean:

  • You have the option of betting on the banker hand, the player hand or a tie.
  • The “banker” and “player” are just names given to the two hands. It’s fine to think of them as “Hand A” and “Hand B” instead.
  • The dealer gives two cards to the banker hand and two cards to the player hand.
  • If either the player or the banker is dealt a total of eight or nine (called a natural), no more cards are dealt.
  • If the player hand has a total of five or less, it receives another card.
  • If the player stands, the banker hand gets another card if it has a total of five or less.
  • In baccarat, when a hand’s score reaches 10 it resets to zero, more on that soon.
  • The hand with the higher total wins or both hands tie.
  • Winning bets on the player and banker pay 1 to 1. Winning bets on a tie pay 8 to 1.
We’ll explain the card values and scoring in more detail below because it’s a little different than what you’re used to in games like blackjack.

The precise rules are actually pretty complicated and are based on the values of both hands as well as the value of the player hand’s third card should they receive one.

Sometimes there are special side bets available in addition to betting on the banker or player. These include the Dragon Bonus, egalities, perfect pair and many more. Look for these bets on the table layout near the regular betting boxes.

#3 – Basic Strategies

If you want to play smart baccarat the single best strategy is to bet on the banker hand every time. In addition to doing that, however, there are a handful of popular betting systems you can use with your banker bet to turbocharge your baccarat session.

Martingale System
This betting system suggests choosing a small bet to start with and then doubling it after every loss. If you win, go back to betting the original amount. The idea with the Martingale is that since the banker will win roughly half the time, you’ll always win back your money plus a small profit when you eventually win. Use this system with caution because a run of losing hands can add up quickly.
Paroli System
The Paroli is essentially the opposite of the Martingale and suggests doubling your bet after a win instead of a loss. First pick your base betting unit and bet one unit to start. When you win, double your bet on the next hand. If you win three in a row, go back to betting one unit. The Paroli system also advises going back to betting one unit after every loss.
1-3-2-4 System
This is another system that lends itself really well to baccarat. First pick your base betting unit and start by betting one unit. Every time you win, move one number to the right in the 1-3-2-4 sequence. Every time you lose, start over by betting one unit. If you win your first two bets, you’re guaranteed a profit. If you win all four bets in a row you profit ten units.

Our Top Baccarat Tips

Generally speaking baccarat is a really simple game but there are a few common mistakes that will cost you big if you keep making them. Follow these rules to give yourself the best chance of winning.

Never Bet on Tie
We covered it earlier but it’s worth repeating: Never bet on tie. The house edge is over 14% on the tie bet compared to 1.06% on the banker hand and 1.24% on the player hand which makes it a no-brainer to avoid.
Never Make Side Bets
Different versions of baccarat offer different side bets but generally speaking they’re all terrible. Side bets like perfect pair, big and small or the dragon bonus all have much higher house edges than betting on the banker.
Don’t Be Superstitious
Baccarat players are notorious for finding patterns and other superstitious reasons to guide their betting. Don’t fall into this trap. Stick to betting on the banker hand and you’ll be guaranteed the best odds in the game.

For a lot of people baccarat isn’t just about getting the best odds. Gamblers love following their intuition and taking risks. Spend some time figuring out what’s important to you and use that to guide your strategy.

Card Counting

Just like in blackjack, the odds in baccarat change depending on which cards are left in the deck. By keeping track of all the cards being dealt, card counters can figure out whether the banker hand or the player hand is more likely to win.

To count cards in baccarat, start with a count of zero. When you see an ace, deuce or three add one to the count. When you see a four, add two to the count. Subtract one from the count when you see a five, seven or eight. Subtract two when you see a six. The higher the count, the more likely the player hand is to win. The lower the count, the more likely the banker hand is to win.

Edge Sorting

Poker pro Phil Ivey made worldwide headlines when he was sued by multiple casinos for edge-sorting. Ivey had won millions of dollars playing baccarat and the casinos alleged he had cheated by exploiting defects in the cards. So what exactly is edge sorting?
When a deck of cards has any subtle difference in the patterns on either edge of the back of the card, clever players can use it to track specific cards. By asking the dealer to orient certain cards one way or the other, the player can see the value of certain cards even when they’re face-down.
It’s an extremely complicated strategy that involves tricking the casino and the dealer into orienting the cards for you. It’s also been proven to be illegal since Phil Ivey has lost both his law-suits after attempting to argue that edge-sorting was within the rules of the game and therefore not cheating.

#8 – History of Baccarat

Baccarat has been played for over half a millennium but it’s only in recent years that it’s become the most popular casino game on the planet. It started in Italy and spread throughout Europe and eventually to North America and Asia. Let’s take a closer look at the major plot points of baccarat’s backstory.

Pre-1400s

Although evidence is scarce, some historians point to the Asian game of Macao as the game that directly inspired baccarat. Macao featured a player and a banker and many other similarities to baccarat. Historians suggest early explorers and merchants brought the game back from China in the days of Marco Polo.

1400s

The game was officially invented in Italy and named “baccara” after the Italian word for zero because all face cards are worth zero. The original game featured four dealers and gave everyone the chance to bet against the bankers or other players.

1800s

Baccarat had spread throughout Europe and became popular in France where they introduced a new version called Chemin de Fer which means “the railroad”. It was so named because it was a faster version of baccarat and, at the time, the railroad was the fastest form of transportation available.

1891

Baccarat received extensive media attention when the Prince of Wales, who would eventually become King Edward VII, sued Sir William Gordon-Cumming for cheating in a game of baccarat at the Prince’s home. Known as the royal baccarat scandal, the court eventually found in favor of the Prince and not only booted Gordon-Cumming from the Scots Guards but also informally banished him from popular society.

1958

Chemin de Fer was first played in a Nevada casino when the Sands opened a table and called the game “chimney”. During the same decade baccarat found its way to Cuba and evolved into Punto Banco. This new version returned to Las Vegas in 1959 and infamously lost the Sands Casino $250,000 on its first night being offered.

2000s

Baccarat was offered online and became the most profitable game in both Las Vegas and Macau. 24 variations of the game were approved by the Nevada Gaming Control Board and it began to account for more casino profit than any other game, including blackjack.

Baccarat used to be an exclusive game reserved for high rollers but nowadays it’s just as accessible as any other casino game.
